AutoRefine: Search and Refine During Think

Yaorui Shi et al.  ·  NeurIPS 2025  ·  2025

📄 arXiv:2503.19823 📥 PDF 💻 Code
TL;DR
AutoRefine integrates retrieval into the reasoning chain itself, enabling LLMs to search and verify intermediate conclusions during thinking — resulting in more faithful and accurate reasoning.
🔍
Motivation

Large Language Models often commit to incorrect reasoning paths without the ability to self-correct mid-chain. Existing retrieval-augmented approaches only retrieve before or after reasoning, missing the opportunity to refine intermediate steps where errors actually occur.

⚙️
Method

AutoRefine introduces a Retrieve-and-Refine mechanism that triggers targeted searches at critical points within the reasoning chain, verifying and correcting intermediate conclusions before they propagate.

1
Uncertainty Detection — monitors the reasoning chain for low-confidence intermediate conclusions using a learned confidence estimator
2
Targeted Retrieval — when uncertainty is detected, triggers focused searches for evidence supporting or contradicting the current reasoning step
3
Step-wise Refinement — integrates retrieved evidence to correct or confirm the intermediate conclusion, then continues the reasoning chain from the refined state
📊
Results

AutoRefine consistently outperforms both vanilla reasoning and retrieve-then-reason baselines across mathematical, multi-hop, and commonsense reasoning benchmarks.

⚠️
Limitations
Retrieval latency increases end-to-end inference time by ~40%, which may be prohibitive for real-time applications
The confidence estimator requires task-specific calibration data, limiting zero-shot deployment
Performance gains diminish on tasks requiring creative or divergent reasoning where retrieval evidence is sparse
✨
Core Takeaway
Retrieving during reasoning is fundamentally more effective than retrieving before reasoning — mid-chain verification catches errors where they actually occur.
